HAVING thrown out the kings, queens and knaves, remaining cards; place the rejected court cards below the cut, so that they shall be the last cards of the pack. Then take the cards one by one, commencing from the top, and lay out upon the table three rows of four cards each, face upwards as shown in the figure.

If among the cards so exposed there are two (irrespective of suit or position) whose pips added together make eleven, you will place upon these two the two uppermost cards of the stock, and proceed in the same manner until (if you are lucky) the twelve court cards, the last of the stock, have been fully distributed, each covering one of the twelve heaps.*
