Glossary

Auxiliary heap –

Dead cards – Any cards that are unused in a particular patience game.

Deuce – A 2 card of any suit.

Foundation – A stack of cards that is required to be filled to win certain patience games.

Knave – Another name for the jack card.

Patience – The British English name for solitaire.

Pip – An element used for counting, in this book each individual spade, diamond, heart or club in the centre of a playing card is referred to as a pip.

Piquet – A popular trick-taking card game in the 19th century that uses a reduced deck with 32 cards consisting of the 7, 8, 9, 10, king, queen, jack and ace of each suit.

Tableau (plural Tableaux) – A vertical stack of cards.

Talon – The stock of a given patience game where more cards are drawn from.

Viz. – An abbreviation for the Latin word videlicet and is used as a formal substitute for namely or in other words.

Waste-heap – An area where cards are discarded from play due to a successful action, for example, after forming pairs.

Whist – A popular trick-taking card game in the 19th century that uses a standard 52 card deck.
