8. Cæsar

(With nine cards of a whist pack.)

SELECT from a whist pack nine cards, namely, an ace, a two, a three, a four, a five, a six, a seven, an eight and a nine. The rest of the pack are thrown aside as "dead" cards.

Of your nine cards, make three rows of three each, one above the other, endeavouring so to arrange them that the number of "pips" in each row, horizontally, vertically, or obliquely from right to left or left to right, shall invariably make the total "fifteen".

You may transpose the cards as often as you please till you obtain the desired result.*

* For the solution, see Solution of the Cæsar Problem
