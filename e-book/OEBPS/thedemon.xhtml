7. The Demon

(With a single whist pack.)

SHUFFLE, cut, and from the top of the pack count off thirteen cards, which place on the table in a packet, face upwards. These consistitute the “stock”. The next four cards are laid out in a row, also face upwards, and are known as the “head of columns”. The next card dealt is called a “base”, and is laid, somewhat inappropriately in view of its name, just above the first column. The three corresponding cards (e.g., three knaves if the first base be a knave), are in like manner placed, as they come to hand, over the heads of the other three columns, and likewise become bases. The cards remaining in hand are the “reserve”.

The object of the player is to build sequences upwards from the bases in their proper suits; and downwards from the heads of columns in alternate colours. Should the cards of one column fit on to another, so to speak, i.e., should the top card, for the time being, of one column be the next lower than the bottom card of another column, and of different colour, the former column is joined on to the latter, the top card of the stock being transferred to the vacant spot to start a fresh column. In like manner, the top card of either column, if appropriate, may be played to either of the bases. It is best to make the cards of the columns overlaps, but in the case of the bases, the higher card should be laid squarely on the top of the lower.

In this case the eight of hearts may at once be played upon the nine of clubs, its place being supplied by the king of hearts, the top card of the stock. As soon as the knave of spades appears the queen of spades will be laid upon it.
